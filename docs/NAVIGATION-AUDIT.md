‚úÖ **SEO Advantage** - Resources drive organic traffic (engineers googling installation guides)

## WordPress Legacy Analysis (Reference Only)

### WordPress Structure Issues:
- ‚ùå **26 items in flat primary menu** - Overwhelming, no hierarchy
- ‚ùå **Mixed concerns** - Products, resources, company all intermingled  
- ‚ùå **No visual cues** - Text-only navigation
- ‚ùå **Poor mobile UX** - Long dropdown lists
- ‚ùå **No featured content** - No calls-to-action or promotions

### Modernization Strategy Applied

**Decision: 4-Column Structure (Products | Resources | Support | Company)**

**Rationale:**
1. **Resources elevated** - B2B buyers need technical docs prominently
2. **Support streamlined** - Focus on customer service, not documentation
3. **Clear separation** - Products = shop, Resources = learn, Support = help, Company = trust
4. **Phase 2 ready** - Learning Center has placeholder links for future video content

**What We Kept from WordPress:**
‚úÖ All product categories  
‚úÖ Application Notes, Service Bulletins  
‚úÖ BAPI Sensors Overview, Product Catalog  
‚úÖ RMA Request, Where to Buy  
‚úÖ Company pages (Why BAPI, Mission, Careers, News)

**What We Improved:**
‚úÖ **Organization** - Grouped by user intent, not flat list  
‚úÖ **Visual hierarchy** - Icons, descriptions, badges  
‚úÖ **Featured panels** - CTAs for each section  
‚úÖ **Mobile-first** - Collapsible mega menu sections  
‚úÖ **SEO** - Resources as main nav item = better crawling/indexing

**What We Deferred:**
‚è∏Ô∏è **Videos** - Content not ready (Phase 2 placeholder added)  
‚è∏Ô∏è **Webinars** - Future training initiative (Phase 2)  
‚è∏Ô∏è **ETA product line** - Niche category, low traffic  
‚è∏Ô∏è **Applications section** - Awaiting marketing content (commented in config)
